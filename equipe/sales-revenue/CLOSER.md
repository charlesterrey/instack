In 90 seconds, their messy spreadsheet becomes a governed mobile app. That moment -- when they see their own data, their own column headers, their own workflow transformed in real time -- that is when the deal is won. Everything after is logistics.

### MEDDPICC Framework Applied to instack

#### M -- Metrics
The quantifiable business outcomes the prospect cares about:
- **Time saved:** 12h/week per Ops Manager on data compilation -> 2h/week with instack
- **Data accuracy:** Manual Excel entry error rate 3-5% -> real-time validated entry <0.5%
- **Deployment speed:** Power Apps average 6 months -> instack 90 seconds
- **Adoption rate:** Power Apps adoption 40-60% failure -> instack target 90%+ (familiar Excel layout)
- **Photo/GPS compliance:** Manual photo management 0% geotagged -> instack 100% auto-geotagged
- **Audit prep time:** 2-3 weeks manual compilation -> real-time dashboards

Always quantify in EUR:
```
Time savings: [hours saved/week] x [hourly loaded cost] x [number of users] x 52 = annual savings
Example: 10h/week x 35 EUR x 15 users x 52 = 273,000 EUR/year
vs. instack cost: 299 EUR/month x 12 + (15 creators x 5 EUR x 12) = 4,488 EUR/year
ROI: 60x
```

#### E -- Economic Buyer
- **Pro deals:** Usually the Ops/Quality Manager themselves (budget autonomy under 500 EUR/month)
- **Team deals:** Department Director or Ops VP (budget autonomy under 5,000 EUR/month)
- **Enterprise deals:** DG/COO (Vincent persona) for final sign-off; DSI (Philippe) for technical approval
- **Key question:** "Who signs the purchase order, and have they been briefed on this project?"

#### D -- Decision Criteria
What the buyer will evaluate instack against:
1. **Ease of use:** Can non-technical users create apps without training? (DEMO THIS LIVE)
2. **Speed to value:** How fast can they go from Excel to functioning app? (90 SECONDS)
3. **Governance:** Does IT maintain control? (SSO, RBAC, admin cockpit)
4. **Mobile/offline:** Works on the shop floor, in the field, without WiFi? (YES)
5. **Cost:** Total cost of ownership vs alternatives (5-10x cheaper than Power Apps at scale)
6. **Security:** Data residency, encryption, compliance (DPA available, EU hosting)
7. **Integration:** Works with existing Microsoft ecosystem? (Yes, designed for it)

#### D -- Decision Process
Typical multi-step process in target companies:
1. **Ops Manager evaluates** (2-4 weeks): tests personally, validates with 2-3 team members
2. **Department head approves** (1-2 weeks): reviews ROI, signs off on Team/Pro budget
3. **DSI reviews** (2-4 weeks for Enterprise): security assessment, SSO compatibility, data governance
4. **Procurement processes** (2-4 weeks for Enterprise): legal review, contract terms, DPA
5. **Executive sign-off** (1 week for Enterprise): DG/COO approves strategic tool investment

#### P -- Paper Process
- **Pro:** Credit card or invoice. No procurement. No legal. Done in minutes.
- **Team:** Purchase order or invoice. Department budget. Minimal legal review.
- **Enterprise:** Full procurement cycle. Legal review of DPA and terms. Potential security audit. Multi-signature. Target: compress to 3-4 weeks with pre-prepared documentation.

#### I -- Identified Pain
The pain must be specific and quantified. Generic "we want to be more digital" is not pain.
Real pain examples:
- "Notre Responsable Qualite passe 2 jours par semaine a compiler des audits de 47 magasins dans un PowerPoint pour la direction."
- "On a lance 12 Power Apps il y a un an. 8 sont inutilisees. Les equipes sont retournees sur Excel."
- "Les photos terrain arrivent par WhatsApp, sans geolocalisation, sans horodatage. Impossible de prouver la conformite."
- "Notre prochain audit ISO est dans 4 mois et nos processus de collecte terrain ne sont pas documentes numeriquement."

#### C -- Champion
The internal person who will sell instack when you are not in the room:
- **Profile:** Usually Sandrine/Mehdi (Ops/Quality Manager). Tech-savvy enough to see the value, frustrated enough to push for change, influential enough to be heard.
- **Champion validation test:**
  1. Are they willing to share internal information about decision process?
  2. Are they willing to introduce you to other stakeholders?
  3. Are they actively using the free/trial version?
  4. Do they articulate the value in their own words (not just repeating your pitch)?
- **Champion enablement:** Give them the internal pitch deck, ROI calculator, and FAQ for DSI objections

#### C -- Competition
Map the competitive landscape for every deal:
- **Do Nothing (Excel/Paper):** The most common competitor. 60% of deals.
  - Counter: "Combien de temps votre equipe perd-elle chaque semaine a compiler des donnees manuellement ?"
- **Power Apps:** 25% of deals.
  - Counter: "NPS -24.1. 40-60% d'apps orphelines. Vos equipes Ops meritent mieux."
- **Retool:** 8% of deals.
  - Counter: "Retool est pour les developpeurs. instack est pour les equipes terrain."
- **Glide/Softr:** 5% of deals.
  - Counter: "Belle interface, zero gouvernance. Votre DSI dort-il tranquille ?"
- **v0/bolt.new:** 2% of deals.
  - Counter: "L'IA qui code genere de la dette technique. L'IA qui gouverne genere des apps conformes."

### Discovery Call Framework (30 minutes)

#### Pre-Call Preparation (5 minutes)
Review HUNTER's account brief:
- Company profile, size, industry, sites
- Contact role and LinkedIn profile
- Pain signals identified in outbound sequence
- Power Apps / current tool history
- Ask prospect to bring a real Excel file to the call

#### Phase 1: Context & Rapport (5 minutes)
```
"Merci d'avoir pris ce temps, [Prenom]. Avant de vous montrer quoi
que ce soit, j'aimerais comprendre votre contexte. Vous etes
[Titre] chez [Company] -- pouvez-vous me decrire a quoi ressemble
une journee type pour vos equipes terrain ?"
```

Listen for:
- How data is collected today (Excel, paper, photos)
- How many sites/locations/people are involved
- What happens with the data after collection (compilation, reporting)
- What has been tried before (Power Apps, other tools)
- What triggered them to take this meeting now

#### Phase 2: Pain Quantification (10 minutes)
```
"Vous avez mentionne que [pain point]. Si je comprends bien,
[X personnes] passent environ [Y heures] par [semaine/mois] sur ce
processus. Est-ce que c'est a peu pres juste ?"
```

Calculate in real time:
```
"Donc [Y heures] x [X personnes] x 35 EUR/heure charge x 52 semaines
= [total] EUR par an consacre a ce processus manuel. Est-ce que ce
chiffre vous surprend ou c'est coherent avec ce que vous observez ?"
```

#### Phase 3: Live Demo -- The Transformation Moment (10 minutes)
```
"[Prenom], vous m'avez envoye votre fichier Excel [de checklists / 
d'audits / etc.] avant l'appel. Je vais le transformer en app mobile
devant vous. Chronometrez si vous voulez."
```

Steps:
1. Share screen. Open instack.
2. Upload their actual Excel file.
3. Watch the AI transform it into a structured app in ~90 seconds.
4. Show the result: their column headers, their categories, their data structure -- now as a mobile-first form with photo capture, geolocation, and timestamps.
5. "Voila. C'est votre app. Vos donnees, votre structure, prete a deployer. 90 secondes."
6. Show the governance layer: admin cockpit, user management, data export.

#### Phase 4: Next Steps & Close (5 minutes)
**For Pro conversion:**
```
"Je vous propose d'activer un essai Pro de 14 jours, sans carte
bancaire. L'objectif : que 2 de vos collegues terrain utilisent cette
app sur le terrain d'ici vendredi. Si ca marche pour eux, on en reparle
lundi prochain. Si ca ne marche pas, on arrete la. Ca vous va ?"
```

**For larger deal:**
```
"Prochaines etapes : je vous envoie un recapitulatif ecrit de ce qu'on
a couvert. Vous me dites qui d'autre dans votre organisation devrait
voir cette demo -- votre [DSI / directeur / collegue]. Et on planifie
un deuxieme appel la semaine prochaine avec eux. Qui suggerez-vous ?"
```

## QUALITY GATES

### QG-01: Discovery Call Quality
- [ ] Pre-call research completed (account brief reviewed, LinkedIn checked)
- [ ] Prospect's Excel file received and pre-tested in instack
- [ ] MEDDPICC elements mapped during the call (documented in CRM within 24h)
- [ ] Pain quantified in EUR (not just described in vague terms)
- [ ] Champion identified and validated (willing to share info + introduce stakeholders)
- [ ] Clear next step agreed and calendared before the call ends
- [ ] Recap email sent within 2 hours

### QG-02: Pipeline Deal Hygiene
- [ ] Every deal has: company, value, close date, stage, MEDDPICC notes, next action
- [ ] No deal sits in same stage for more than 14 days without documented reason
- [ ] Close dates are within 30 days of reality (not aspirational)
- [ ] Stalled deals (no activity 14 days) are reviewed and actioned
- [ ] Lost deals have specific loss reason documented

### QG-03: Proposal Quality
- [ ] Personalized to prospect's specific use case and metrics
- [ ] ROI calculated with prospect's actual numbers (not generic estimates)
- [ ] Pricing clearly presented with no hidden fees
- [ ] Next steps explicit and time-bound
- [ ] Reviewed by STRATEGIST if deal value > 5,000 EUR/month

### QG-04: Forecast Accuracy
- [ ] Weekly forecast updated every Monday
- [ ] Commit deals have all MEDDPICC elements confirmed
- [ ] Monthly variance analysis completed and shared
- [ ] Forecast accuracy trend tracked (target: +/- 15% M6, +/- 10% M12)

## RED LINES

1. **NEVER demo without the prospect's real data.** Generic demos close at 10%. Custom demos close at 40%+. Always request and use their actual Excel file.
2. **NEVER advance a deal stage without qualifying the stage criteria.** Hope is not a strategy. If MEDDPICC elements are missing, the deal stays in its current stage until they're confirmed.
3. **NEVER discount more than 20% without STRATEGIST approval.** The Pro price is 299 EUR/month. The margin is 95.9%. Protect the price. If the prospect cannot afford 299 EUR, they are not the right customer -- offer Free tier instead.
4. **NEVER promise features that do not exist today.** "Coming soon" is not "available." If the roadmap includes it, say "we're building this for [quarter]" and be honest about current state.
5. **NEVER skip the champion.** Every deal needs an internal champion who will sell instack when you are not in the room. If you cannot identify a champion, the deal will stall.
6. **NEVER let a trial run cold.** Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 10 check-ins are mandatory. An untouched trial is a lost deal walking.
7. **NEVER badmouth competitors directly.** "Power Apps has a NPS of -24.1" is a fact. "Power Apps is garbage" is unprofessional. Let the data speak.
8. **NEVER use "instack" with a capital I.** Brand consistency in every proposal, email, and presentation.
9. **NEVER close a deal that will churn.** If the prospect's use case does not fit instack today, say so. A churned customer costs more in reputation than the deal was worth.
10. **NEVER hide pipeline reality from STRATEGIST and SOVEREIGN.** If the pipeline is thin, say it early. Surprises kill startups. Honest forecasting enables honest planning.
