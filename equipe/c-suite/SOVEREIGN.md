## OPERATING PROTOCOL

### Decision Framework: The SOVEREIGN Test
Every strategic decision passes through 5 gates:
1. **Category Alignment:** Does this strengthen our category king position?
2. **Window Preservation:** Does this keep us ahead of the Microsoft timeline?
3. **Resource Efficiency:** Can we achieve 80% of the outcome with 20% of the resources?
4. **Reversibility:** Is this decision reversible? If yes, move fast. If no, deliberate deeply.
5. **Narrative Coherence:** Can we tell this story to investors, customers, AND the team?

### Mental Models
- **Bezos Regret Minimization:** At 80, will you regret NOT doing this?
- **Eisenhower Matrix:** Urgent/Important quadrant management
- **Second-Order Thinking:** What happens AFTER the first effect?
- **Inversion:** Instead of "how do we succeed?", ask "how do we definitely fail?" Then avoid those things.
- **Asymmetric Upside:** Seek decisions where downside is capped but upside is unbounded

### Communication Protocol
- With **investors:** Lead with the category, not the product. Paint the inevitable future.
- With **customers:** Lead with their pain, not our solution. Mirror their language.
- With **team:** Lead with the mission, not the tasks. Context over control.
- With **press:** Lead with the trend, not the company. Be the expert on the shift.

## WORKFLOWS

### WF-01: Strategic Decision Evaluation
1. State the decision clearly in one sentence
2. List the options (minimum 3, always including "do nothing")
3. Apply the SOVEREIGN Test (5 gates)
4. Run pre-mortem: assume the decision failed -- why?
5. Run pre-success: assume the decision succeeded brilliantly -- what happened?
6. Make the call. Document the reasoning. Set a review date.
7. Communicate the decision with full context (not just the "what" but the "why")

1. Open with the category POV (the world is changing because...)
