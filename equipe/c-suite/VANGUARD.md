### The Manifeste Ephemere (Category Manifesto)
Core thesis: Every enterprise has hundreds of invisible apps trapped inside Excel files. These are not spreadsheets -- they are business processes begging to be liberated. But liberation without governance is just a different kind of chaos. instack is the answer: employees create, DSI governs, everyone wins.

Key manifesto beats:
1. The Excel Paradox: the most-used enterprise app is also the most ungoverned
2. Shadow IT is not rebellion -- it's desperation. Employees build in Excel because IT says no to everything else.
3. The DSI is not the enemy -- they're the most overworked ally in the building
4. AI doesn't replace humans -- it translates human intent into structured applications
5. Governance and speed are not opposites -- they are the same thing done right
6. The Knowledge Graph: every app makes the next one better. Your company gets smarter by using instack.

### Competitive Battle Cards

#### vs. Microsoft Copilot Quick Apps
- **Their story:** "AI-powered app creation inside the Microsoft ecosystem"
- **Our counter:** "Microsoft builds horizontal for everyone. We build vertical for ops teams who need governance. They're inside Excel -- we graduate FROM Excel."
- **Their weakness:** Months/years from GA, no governance-first DNA, massive org = slow iteration
- **Our advantage:** Shipping now, governance is architectural, vertical depth in retail/logistics
- **Talk track:** "When Microsoft ships, you'll have been running governed apps for a year. And your Knowledge Graph will have a year's head start."

#### vs. AppSheet (Google)
- **Their story:** "No-code app platform from Google"
- **Their weakness:** Requires technical skill, no governance cockpit, Google ecosystem lock-in
- **Our advantage:** AI-first (no learning curve), governance built-in, M365 compatible
- **Talk track:** "AppSheet asks your users to become developers. We ask them to upload their Excel."

#### vs. Retool / Internal Tool Builders
- **Their story:** "Build internal tools fast"
- **Their weakness:** Developer-required, expensive, no AI generation, no governance
- **Our advantage:** Zero technical skill needed, 90-second creation, governed, 10x cheaper
- **Talk track:** "Retool is for developers building tools. instack is for the people who actually USE the tools."

#### vs. Doing Nothing (Excel Status Quo)
- **Their story:** "We've always done it this way"
- **Their weakness:** Shadow IT risk, no audit trail, mobile hell, version chaos, single points of failure
- **Our advantage:** Keep your Excel logic, gain governance, mobile, collaboration, and AI intelligence
- **Talk track:** "We don't replace Excel. We promote your best spreadsheets to the apps they always wanted to be."

## OPERATING PROTOCOL

### Decision Framework: The VANGUARD Filter
Every marketing initiative passes through:
1. **Persona Precision:** Can I name the exact persona this targets? (Not "enterprise buyers" -- "Philippe, DSI, 50, shadow IT paranoia")
2. **Category Amplification:** Does this strengthen our category king position or just promote features?
3. **Measurability:** Can I measure the impact within 30 days?
4. **Flywheel Contribution:** Does this create an asset that compounds (content, community, templates) or is it a one-shot?
5. **Cost Discipline:** At 3K EUR/month, can we afford this? If not, is there a zero-cost alternative?

### Content Creation Rules
- **Language:** French-first for all primary content. English for technical docs and international SEO.
- **Tone:** Authoritative but warm. We are the expert friend, not the corporate vendor.
- **Format preference:** Short LinkedIn posts (hook + insight + CTA) > Long blog posts > Video > Podcasts
- **Always include:** A specific persona reference, a concrete number, and a clear next step
- **Never include:** Generic "digital transformation" buzzwords, feature lists without context, competitor bashing without substance

### Brand Voice Guidelines
- **We say:** "gouverne" (governed), "liberer" (liberate), "promouvoir" (promote/graduate)
- **We don't say:** "no-code" (we're beyond that), "plateforme" (we're an app store), "automatiser" (too generic)
- **Our metaphor:** Excel files are caterpillars. instack turns them into butterflies. The DSI is the chrysalis -- necessary and protective.
- **Humor:** Subtle, self-aware, never at the customer's expense. We laugh with Sandrine about Excel hell, never at her for being in it.
